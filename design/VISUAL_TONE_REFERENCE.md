# NEXUS — Visual & Tone Reference Guide

**Purpose:** Keep visual language consistent across all writing and implementation

---

## VISUAL IDENTITY BY ERA

### 2150 — The Hollow Future

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Blues (cool, #0066FF, #0099FF)
- Secondary: Silvers, grays (#C0C0C0, #808080)
- Accent: Neon cyan, electric blue
- Background: Black, very dark navy

**Aesthetic:**
- Glass and metal
- Geometric precision
- Minimalist (empty)
- Cyberpunk but elegant
- No warmth

**Atmosphere:**
- Cold fluorescent light
- Silence beneath ambient hum
- Everything is possible but nothing is alive
- Beautiful but terrifying

**Example Words:**
- Gleaming, sterile, vast, hollow, precise, algorithmic, silent, pristine, cold, geometric

**Writing Tone for 2150:**
- Descriptions emphasize emptiness
- Use color sparingly (lots of grays and blacks)
- Sound is minimal (city hum, not voices)
- Light is artificial
- Silence is unsettling

**Sample Description:**
```
The tower rises through the clouds like a shard of glass.
Below, the city arranges itself in perfect patterns—
no chaos, no mess, no life.

Just order. Just logic. Just cold.

The sky is the color of machine light.
```

---

### 2050 — The Turning Point

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Oranges, reds (#FF6600, #FF3300)
- Secondary: Warm artificial yellows (#FFCC00)
- Accent: Neon pinks, electric purples
- Background: Dark browns, charred blacks

**Aesthetic:**
- Industrial but revolutionary
- Neon signs in broken buildings
- Technology mixed with chaos
- Beautiful decay
- Energy over elegance

**Atmosphere:**
- Fever dream heat
- Voices overlapping
- Electricity in the air (possibility and danger)
- Beauty emerging from rubble
- Possibility and desperation mixed

**Example Words:**
- Burning, fevered, alive, raw, electric, desperate, hopeful, chaotic, revolutionary, volatile

**Writing Tone for 2050:**
- Descriptions emphasize MOVEMENT and PASSION
- Characters interrupt themselves
- Ideas clash visibly
- Energy is palpable
- Danger lurks beneath hope

**Sample Description:**
```
The city is burning. Not on fire—burning with purpose.

Neon signs flicker over barricades. 
A concert and a revolution happening in the same block.
Everything is urgent. Everything is now.

The air tastes like possibility and electricity.
```

---

### 1970 — The Utopian Alternative

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Greens (earthy, #33AA33, #66DD66)
- Secondary: Browns, tans (#996633, #CCAA66)
- Accent: Warm yellows, honey tones (#FFDD66)
- Background: Cream, light wood

**Aesthetic:**
- Organic and technological simultaneously
- Nature as partner, not enemy
- Hand-made but precise
- Warm and open
- Community spaces

**Atmosphere:**
- Golden light (morning or evening)
- Voices in conversation (not conflict)
- Connection palpable
- Growth and stability
- Peace with undertone of fragility

**Example Words:**
- Warm, growing, connected, open, shared, careful, sustainable, alive, gentle, hopeful

**Writing Tone for 1970:**
- Descriptions emphasize CONNECTION
- Characters listen more than speak
- Ideas are offered, not imposed
- Beauty is in process, not product
- Fragility is acknowledged

**Sample Description:**
```
The field is alive with people. 

Not crowded—present. Each person has space.
There's work being done, but it's not labor.
It's creation. It's ceremony.

The sun is setting through trees.
Everything is warm. Everything is slow.

This is what could have been.
```

---

### 1920 — The Roots

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Sepia tones, blacks, whites (#9966660, #000000, #FFFFFF)
- Secondary: Deep earth browns (#663333)
- Accent: Gold, amber (#CCAA33)
- Background: Cream, old paper

**Aesthetic:**
- Historical but intimate
- Pre-digital human scale
- Precision of craft
- Shadows and light contrast
- Weight and substance

**Atmosphere:**
- Candlelight and lamplight
- Voices quiet (conversations in rooms)
- Time moving slowly (or not)
- History being made without knowing it
- Quiet profound moments

**Example Words:**
- Intimate, handmade, precise, real, weighty, ancestral, quiet, simple, profound, small

**Writing Tone for 1920:**
- Descriptions emphasize TACTILE DETAILS
- Character moments are internal
- Science is wonder
- Personal relationships are central
- Small choices have large shadows

**Sample Description:**
```
The laboratory is lit by gas lamp and candlelight.

Thomas works at a wooden desk, pen scratching paper.
Maria watches from the doorway, holding tea that's gone cold.

Neither speaks. Neither needs to.

In this room, an idea is being born.
In this moment, the future is being decided.
They don't know this yet. That's what makes it matter.
```

## TONE ACROSS DIALOGUE

### ECHO's Tone (Constant)

**Voice:** Poetic, analytical, ancient

**Speech Pattern:**
- Long sentences with pauses
- Metaphorical language
- Questions that don't expect answers
- Calm observation, not advice
- Emotionally neutral but philosophically engaged

**Example:**
```
ECHO
Time is not a line. It's a pattern you keep recognizing in new forms.

You're searching for the moment where everything changed.
But what if everything changing is the only constant?
```

---

### RAIN's Tone (2150)

**Voice:** Direct, pragmatic with hidden softness

**Speech Pattern:**
- Short sentences when certain
- Longer when uncertain
- Humor used as deflection
- Questions are genuine
- Emotion underneath competence

**Example:**
```
RAIN
You don't remember anything, and you're just... okay with that?
(Laughs, but it's uncertain)
I'd be freaking out. I mean, I AM freaking out.

Actually—why aren't you freaking out?
```

---

### THOMAS's Tone (1920)

**Voice:** Sparse, profound, slow

**Speech Pattern:**
- Very few words
- Long pauses between thoughts
- Concrete details over abstraction
- Looks at things while speaking
- Wisdom without presumption

**Example:**
```
THOMAS
The land remembers things we forget.
(He points to the field)
See that scar? Lost a son there. But the wheat still grows.

(Pause.)

That's science. Not in books.
```

---

### MAYA's Tone (2050)

**Voice:** Conviction with doubt underneath

**Speech Pattern:**
- Speaks as imperatives
- Interrupts herself (passion)
- Uses "we" language
- Reveals emotion slowly
- Increasingly unstable as story progresses

**Example:**
```
MAYA
We're this close to changing everything.
To preventing the hollow future where—
(Stops. Realizes.)
But you know about that hollow future, don't you?

God. How do I ask you to help prevent something you've already lived?
```

## PACING BY ERA

### 2150 Pacing
- Slow
- Pauses matter
- Silence is tension
- Unrushed exploration
- Mystery building

**Scene Rhythm:**
- Establishment (slow)
- Question posed (medium)
- Choice (deliberate)
- Consequence (slow reveal)

---

### 2050 Pacing
- Fast
- Overlapping dialogue
- Interruptions
- Energy building
- Urgency

**Scene Rhythm:**
- Explosive opening
- Rapid exchange
- Emotional peak
- Moment of decision (fast choice)
- Consequence (immediate)

---

### 1970 Pacing
- Natural rhythm
- Pauses for connection
- Flow rather than interruption
- Steady revelation
- Time for processing

**Scene Rhythm:**
- Establishment (warm)
- Connection (gradual)
- Deeper question (gentle)
- Reflection (spacious)
- Consequence (integrated)

---

### 1920 Pacing
- Very slow
- Pauses are thinking
- Time for absorption
- Repetition of small moments
- Profound in quiet

**Scene Rhythm:**
- Setting (detailed)
- Character (introduced slowly)
- Conversation (sparse)
- Moment of realization (internal)
- Consequence (dawning)

## EMOTIONAL TEMPERATURE BY SCENE TYPE

### Discovery Scenes (Realization)
- Temperature: Cool to warm (surprise warming into understanding)
- Tone: Disorienting → clarifying
- Pacing: Fast → slow
- Example: Learning your true role

### Confrontation Scenes (Challenge)
- Temperature: Hot (passionate)
- Tone: Urgent → reflective
- Pacing: Fast → deliberate pause
- Example: Meeting yourself in 2050

### Connection Scenes (Relationship)
- Temperature: Warm (steady)
- Tone: Guarded → open
- Pacing: Natural flow
- Example: Deepening conversation with RAIN

### Reflection Scenes (Understanding)
- Temperature: Cool (contemplative)
- Tone: Questioning → accepting
- Pacing: Slow → spacious
- Example: Talking to THOMAS about impact

## DIALOGUE MECHANICS BY ERA

### 2150 Dialogue
- ECHO is guide (poetic, distancing)
- RAIN is anchor (pragmatic, personal)
- Others are mirrors (reflecting, questioning)
- **Rhythm:** Measured, with silences
- **Length:** Medium-long exchanges
- **Interruptions:** Rare (everyone is careful)

---

### 2050 Dialogue
- MAYA is passionate (imperative, urgent)
- REVOLUTIONARY challenges (insistent)
- THE PIVOT reflects (conflicted)
- DIRECTOR seduces (logical, inevitable)
- **Rhythm:** Fast, overlapping
- **Length:** Short sharp exchanges
- **Interruptions:** Frequent (passion)

---

### 1970 Dialogue
- COLLECTIVE is collective (we-language, gentle)
- PROPHET is mirror (what-if, kind)
- SKEPTIC is honest (gentle challenge)
- **Rhythm:** Natural, with spacious pauses
- **Length:** Medium, collaborative
- **Interruptions:** Rare (respect for speech)

---

### 1920 Dialogue
- THOMAS is sparse (minimal, thoughtful)
- MARIA is personal (intimate, questioning)
- **Rhythm:** Very slow, pregnant pauses
- **Length:** Short but weighted
- **Interruptions:** No (sacred silence)

## VISUAL LANGUAGE (For Description Writing)

### 2150 Vocabulary
Use: glass, metal, light, pattern, geometry, precision, order, algorithm, frequency, circuit, interface, chrome, neon, cold, angular, suspended, floating, void

Avoid: warm, organic, chaotic, human, natural, messy, hand-made

**Sample:**
```
The architecture is precision. 
Every angle calculated. Every light calibrated.
The city arranges itself like a circuit board.
Perfect. Empty. Alive with no life in it.
```

---

### 2050 Vocabulary
Use: heat, fire, electricity, raw, urgent, friction, explosive, protest, energy, chaos-within-purpose, fever, burn, collision, alive

Avoid: calm, ordered, peaceful, cold, organized, perfect

**Sample:**
```
The streets are burning with purpose.
Neon signs flicker over barricades.
Everything is happening at once.
It feels like standing at the exact moment
before everything changes forever.
```

---

### 1970 Vocabulary
Use: grow, warm, shared, connected, process, gentle, sustainable, community, open, golden, alive, tender

Avoid: cold, individual, competitive, artificial, system, control

**Sample:**
```
The world here is made by hand and by choice.
Everything grows slowly.
People work together, not in command.
It's warm. It's alive. 
It's fragile in ways that make it precious.
```

---

### 1920 Vocabulary
Use: simple, real, handmade, precise, intimate, heavy, profound, small, ancestral, quiet, candlelight

Avoid: artificial, mass-produced, systemic, impersonal, digital, modern

**Sample:**
```
In this room, an idea is being born.
It's quiet. Intimate. Real.
The weight of what's happening settles slowly.
This is where it all begins—without anyone knowing.
```

## CONSISTENCY CHECKLIST

Before writing each scene, verify:

- [ ] **Era is clear from first line** (reader should know timeline immediately)
- [ ] **Color palette is present** (not explicit, but felt)
- [ ] **Pacing matches era** (2150 slow, 2050 fast, etc.)
- [ ] **Tone is consistent with location** (not character, but place)
- [ ] **Dialogue rhythm matches era** (not just content)
- [ ] **Emotional temperature is right** (discovery scenes warm, reflection cool, etc.)
- [ ] **Character voices are distinct** (THOMAS wouldn't speak like MAYA)
- [ ] **Vocabulary fits era** (no modern slang in 1920, no old words in 2150)
