# Act 3 Core Philosophy

## The Progression

**Act 1:**
> Amnésia não é inocência.

The player discovers that not remembering does not make them clean.

**Act 2:**
> Culpa não é propriedade.

The player discovers they were not the only one, but that does not absolve them.

**Act 3:**
> Reparo não é posse.

The player must discover that wanting to fix something does not grant ownership over it.

---

# Central Question of Act 3

> Quem tem direito de restaurar aquilo que outros sobreviveram perdendo?

This is the question that breaks the symmetry of the first two acts.

In Act 1 and 2, the player was asking about themselves and their guilt.

In Act 3, the player must ask about others' rights—including the right to refuse repair.

---

# Emotional Arc of Act 3

```
Desire to Repair
  ↓
Resistance From The Repaired
  ↓
The Cost of Restoration
  ↓
The Name That Refuses Return
  ↓
Shared Repair or Chosen Ruin
```

The player enters Act 3 wanting finally to do something right.

**That is the danger.**

After two acts of debt and responsibility, they may confuse repair with redemption.

Act 3 must prevent this.

---

# Player State at Act 3 Start

## Knowledge
- Not innocent
- Not sole culprit
- Cannot carry everything alone
- Must ask before touching
- Must accept refusal

## What They Still Don't Understand
Until the end of Act 3, the player does not fully grasp that:

**Even repair can be a form of control.**

---

# Fourth Line at Act 3 Start

**Status:** `unstable_shared_glow`

The fourth line is present but unstable.

It represents: **vínculo + responsabilidade compartilhada** (shared bond + shared responsibility)

It still cannot complete because the player has not yet faced the final temptation:

**Restoring something against the will of those who survived its loss.**

---

# Fourth Line Completion Rules

The fourth line can only stabilize if the player accepts one of these truths:

1. **Not all restoration is care**
2. **Not all loss demands reversal**
3. **Not all people want to return to what they were**

## Possible Final States

### State A: Completed Shared Line
```text
fourth_line_status = "completed_shared_line"
```

**Condition:**
The player chooses to repair only with consent, accepts irreversible losses, and does not force return of names/memories.

**Philosophy:**
Repair is possible, but only as shared decision-making without coercion.

---

### State B: Chosen Incompletion
```text
fourth_line_status = "chosen_incompletion"
```

**Condition:**
The player refuses to complete the line, accepting that incompletion can also be a form of integrity.

**Philosophy:**
This may be the strongest ending philosophically. Some things should remain open questions.

---

### State C: Forced Completion
```text
fourth_line_status = "forced_completion"
```

**Condition:**
The player forces restoration in the name of justice without sufficient consent.

**Philosophy:**
The symbol completes, but as a scar forcibly closed. Not victory. Control with the aesthetic of cure.

---

### State D: Contained Glow
```text
fourth_line_status = "contained_glow"
```

**Condition:**
The player maintains containment with gradual reforms, refusing both restoration and destruction.

**Philosophy:**
Pragmatic but incomplete. The line glows within constraints.

# Main Locations of Act 3

## Location 1: Deep City — The Names That Refuse Return

The Deep City is no longer just an archive.

It is a living community.

- Some people want restoration
- Some want memory
- Some want names returned
- Others survived precisely because they lost everything

**Act 3 begins here.**

---

## Location 2: 2150 Surface — Continuity Infrastructure

The player sees how LYRA's systems sustain real lives.

Turning off or reversing the system may free some and destroy others.

**Key insight:** The cage also holds the roof.

---

## Location 3: 1920 — Mara Revisited

Return to Mara.

Not to ask for answers.

To face the origin of the question:

> If I created the possibility of restoring memory, do I have the right to decide when it should return?

Mara must challenge the player again—not as guide, but as limit.

---

## Location 4: 2050 Echo Layer — The System That Worked

Voss, Orren, and Ren return as echoes of decision.

Not to repeat the past, but to show:

**The system did not survive because it was false. It survived because it worked well enough.**

---

## Location 5: Nexus Core — The Restoration Engine

The player discovers that the Nexus Machine can restore names, memories, or lines of possibility.

But restoration requires choosing:

- What returns
- Who decides
- Who pays the cost
- Who remains lost

# Key Returning Characters

## Ivo — The Guardian

**Function:** Guard the names that should not be taken

**Position:** Against mass restoration

**Why:** Not because he loves forgetting, but because he understands that names returned without context become violence.

**Core Line:**
> Um nome devolvido sem pergunta vira coleira.
> (A name returned without asking becomes a collar.)

---

## Sera — The Proof

**Function:** Live proof that not being restored is a legitimate choice

**Position:** Central to Act 3. She proves that "healing" someone can erase the way they survived.

**Core Line:**
> Eu não sou uma versão quebrada de alguém que você acha que deveria voltar.
> (I am not a broken version of someone you think should return.)

---

## Lia — The Refusal Echo

**Function:** Echo of collective refusal

**Appearance:** Memory, recording, caderno note, or possible return to 1970

**Question:** Can you accept that a possible future chose not to continue?

**Core Line:**
> Nem toda semente quer virar floresta.
> (Not every seed wants to become a forest.)

---

## Mara — The Origin Limit

**Function:** Origin that refuses ownership of the origin

**Character Arc:** More tired, perhaps after Act 1 events

**Knowledge:** She knows something in 2150 wants to restore names in mass

**Rejection:** She rejects total responsibility

**Core Line:**
> Criar uma porta não me deu direito sobre quem atravessou.
> (Creating a door did not give me rights over who crossed it.)

---

## LYRA — The Learning System

**Function:** System of containment learning to ask

**Evolution:** Should evolve beyond simple ally

**Philosophy:** Understanding that containment without consent is prison, but total absence of containment can also be abandonment

**Core Line:**
> Talvez cuidado seja perguntar antes de fechar a porta. Talvez seja ficar quando ela permanece aberta.
> (Perhaps care is asking before closing the door. Perhaps it is staying when it remains open.)

---

## ECHO — Memory Without Ownership

**Function:** Memory without property rights

**Clarity:** Becomes clearer but not entirely trustworthy

**Danger:** Wants connection, but too much connection can dissolve boundaries

**Core Line:**
> Tudo pode voltar. Essa sempre foi a mentira mais bonita.
> (Everything can return. That was always the most beautiful lie.)

---

## NEXUS — The Overloaded Pattern

**Function:** Memory pattern burdened by many restoration desires

**Capacity:** Technically capable of restoration at some level

**Limitation:** But restoration is not morally simple

**Core Line:**
> Restaurar é escolher uma versão da perda para chamar de erro.
> (To restore is to choose one version of loss to call an error.)

---

# New Characters for Act 3

## Mael — The Desire to Return

**Function:** Resident of Deep City who wants their erased name restored

**Position:** Represents the legitimate desire to be restored

**Core Line:**
> Eu não sobrevivi para virar argumento contra quem quer lembrar.
> (I did not survive to become an argument against those who want to remember.)

---

## Nia — The Right to Remain Changed

**Function:** Resident of Deep City who refuses restoration

**Position:** Lost something and built a life around that loss

**Core Line:**
> Não devolva uma pessoa que eu precise enterrar de novo.
> (Don't return a person I would need to bury again.)

---

## The Restoration Faction

**Function:** Group in 2150 that believes all erased names and memories should be restored

**Characterization:** Not villains. Responding to real violence.

**Risk:** Their movement could become an inverted mirror of containment

**Slogan:**
> O QUE FOI TOMADO DEVE VOLTAR.
> (What was taken must return.)

**Counter-Question:**
> Mesmo quando voltar também toma algo?
> (Even when returning also takes something?)

# Act 3 Ending Philosophy

Act 3 should **not** say:

> Restoration is good.

Act 3 should **not** say:

> Restoration is bad.

Act 3 should say:

> Restoration without consent is another form of taking.

And:

> Refusing restoration can be care, grief, fear, wisdom, or survival.

---

## The Final Question

The player does not become whole by restoring everything.

The world does not become healed by returning to what it was.

**Some names must come back.**

**Some must wait.**

**Some must be asked.**

**Some must remain lost because the people who survived their absence have the right to say so.**

The final question is not:

> Did you fix the future?

The final question is:

> Did you learn to ask before calling something broken?
