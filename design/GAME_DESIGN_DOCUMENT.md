## 1. HIGH CONCEPT

**Title:** NEXUS: The Journey Through Time

**Elevator Pitch:**  
A narrative adventure game where you travel through parallel universes across different time periods to uncover your personal legacy and understand what it means to exist across all timelines.

**Core Theme:**  
*"We are all echoes in time, searching for resonance."*

---

## 2. WORLD SETTING

### Timeline Overview

The game exists in 4 parallel universes representing different historical branches:

#### **2150 — The Hollow Future (Primary Timeline)**
- **Setting:** Mega-cities dominated by AI
- **Atmosphere:** Cold, sleek, disconnected
- **Technology Level:** Advanced (holograms, neural interfaces, autonomous systems)
- **Social Structure:** Highly stratified; AI makes most decisions
- **Key Question:** "What happened to human agency?"
- **Player Start:** You wake here with no memories

#### **2050 — The Turning Point (Branching Timeline)**
- **Setting:** The moment when humanity made the CRUCIAL CHOICE
- **Atmosphere:** Chaotic, revolutionary, charged with tension
- **Technology Level:** Modern+30 years (experimental but still tangible)
- **Social Structure:** Fractured between those choosing AI integration and those resisting
- **Key Question:** "When did we lose ourselves?"
- **Significance:** You discover yourself here — a pivotal figure in this era

#### **1970 — The Utopian Alternative (If-Timeline)**
- **Setting:** A world where humanity chose community over control
- **Atmosphere:** Warm, organic, deeply connected
- **Technology Level:** Advanced but humanized (bio-tech, symbiotic AI)
- **Social Structure:** Flat hierarchies, shared decision-making
- **Key Question:** "What could we have become?"
- **Significance:** Shows the road NOT taken; explores lost potential

#### **1920 — The Roots (Origin Timeline)**
- **Setting:** The beginning of modern era; pre-automation
- **Atmosphere:** Simple, intimate, real human struggles
- **Technology Level:** Early 20th century
- **Social Structure:** Communities, families, personal relationships
- **Key Question:** "Where does it all begin?"
- **Significance:** You find the original pattern that repeats through time

## 3. NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### Three Acts + Epilogue

#### **ACT 1: AWAKENING (2150)**
**Duration:** ~2 hours

You wake in 2150 with fragmented memories. The city is beautiful but hollow. NPCs acknowledge you vaguely — "Don't I know you?" — but can't quite place you.

**Key Beats:**
1. Opening scene: Waking in a glass tower
2. Exploration of 2150: Learning the world
3. Discovery: "You've been here before"
4. Finding the Machine: Time travel device hidden in archives
5. First Choice: "Will you seek the truth?"
6. Entering 2050

**Narrative Goal:** Establish the mystery and introduce the concept of parallel universes.

**Memorable Moment:** A moment where an NPC almost recognizes you but can't — creating cognitive dissonance.

---

#### **ACT 2: RECOGNITION (2050 + 1970)**
**Duration:** ~3-4 hours

You jump to 2050 and are SHOCKED — people know exactly who you are. You're famous/infamous. A pivotal figure.

**Sequence:**
1. Arrival in 2050: "Wait... I'm here? I exist here?"
2. Learning your role: What did this version of you do?
3. Key Meeting: Meet yourself (or a version that shaped this timeline)
4. The Crucial Choice: Understanding THE moment
5. Divergence: Jump to 1970 to see the alternative
6. Recognition in Utopia: This world exists BECAUSE of 2050 choices
7. Return to 2050: Deeper understanding

**Narrative Goal:** Reveal that you're not a passive observer — you're the catalyst.

**Emotional Peak:** Meeting yourself and realizing you're both victim and architect.

---

#### **ACT 3: UNDERSTANDING (1920 + Reflection)**
**Duration:** ~2 hours

You go to 1920 and find the PATTERN — the original choice that cascades through time.

**Sequence:**
1. Arrival in 1920: A small moment that doesn't seem important
2. Key Relationship: Meeting the person who started it all
3. The Original Choice: What small decision echoes forward?
4. Echoes Everywhere: Seeing how 1920 connects to all timelines
5. The Paradox: You realize you can't change anything — only understand it
6. Return to Present: With full knowledge

**Narrative Goal:** Shift from "Can I change the past?" to "What does it mean I'm here at all?"

**Thematic Moment:** Realizing that understanding IS the victory, not changing anything.

---

#### **EPILOGUE: LEGACY**
**Duration:** ~30 minutes

Back in 2150, you see it differently now. The Epilogue has multiple endings based on:
- Who you connected with most
- Which echoes you prioritized
- What "meaning" you chose to assign

**Possible Endings:**
1. **The Connector** — You realize meaning comes from relationships
2. **The Architect** — You accept your role in shaping history
3. **The Witness** — You find peace in simply observing and understanding
4. **The Paradox** — You embrace the contradiction of existence

## 4. CHARACTER ARCHETYPES

### The Four Selves

You don't have a single "character." You are:

1. **The Seeker (2150)** — Confused, searching, desperate for meaning
2. **The Pivot (2050)** — Confident, influential, but weighed by choices
3. **The Echo (1970)** — A ghost in someone else's utopia, seeing what could be
4. **The Origin (1920)** — Innocent, before knowledge, the first choice

**Character Development:** You don't become someone — you integrate all four selves into a complete understanding.

### Key NPCs

Each universe has 3-4 key NPCs:

- **The Companion** — Someone who travels with you, asks questions
- **The Mirror** — Someone who shows you yourself from outside
- **The Oracle** — Someone who knows more than they're saying
- **The Consequence** — Someone whose life was changed by your choices

## 5. GAMEPLAY MECHANICS

### Dialogue & Choices

**System:**
- Branching dialogue tree
- Choices affect NPC relationships
- Some choices lock out future options
- No "right" answers — consequences, not morality

**Example:**
```
COMPANION: "Do you think we should tell them the truth?"

CHOICE A: "Yes, people deserve to know" 
  → NPCs trust you more BUT become anxious
  
CHOICE B: "No, ignorance is kinder"
  → NPCs are happier BUT resent you when truth emerges
  
CHOICE C: [Say nothing, just leave]
  → NPCs never trust you fully
```

### Memory/Echo System

**Mechanics:**
- Every NPC conversation creates an "Echo"
- Echoes are fragments of who this person is
- Collect enough echoes → unlock memories
- Memories reveal connections across timelines

**Example:**
```
Echo: "MAYA — The activist who believed in change"

After 3 conversations with Maya across universes:
MEMORY UNLOCKED: "Maya's Choice"
You see the moment Maya made the crucial decision in 2050
This memory is now accessible in 1920 to understand the origin
```

### Time Travel Mechanic

**How it Works:**
- You don't "go back in time" — you enter alternate universes
- Each universe remembers previous visits from you
- Changes you make don't alter past — they reveal what was always true
- Puzzle-like: Revisit timelines with new knowledge to understand layers

**Example:**
```
First visit to 1920: 
  "A farmer named THOMAS works the land"
  
After learning his role in 2050:
  Revisit 1920: 
  "THOMAS — the ancestor of the AI architect"
  New dialogue options appear
```

### Save System

- **Auto-save:** Each universe change saves
- **Manual save:** Anytime during dialogue
- **Multiple saves:** Can have different playthroughs
- **New Game+:** Option to replay knowing the story

## 6. TONE & STYLE

### Visual Style
- **Aesthetic:** Cyberpunk (2150/2050) + Pastoral (1970/1920)
- **Color Palette:**
  - 2150: Blues, silvers, neon (cold)
  - 2050: Oranges, reds, warm artificial (transition)
  - 1970: Greens, earth tones (organic)
  - 1920: Sepia, blacks, whites (historical)

### Narrative Tone
- **Philosophical** — Big questions without easy answers
- **Intimate** — Personal relationships matter more than world-ending stakes
- **Melancholic** — Acceptance that some things can't be changed
- **Hopeful** — Understanding brings peace

### Writing Style
- Long, thoughtful monologues
- Dialogue that reveals character through what's NOT said
- Poetic descriptions of settings
- Questions posed to the player without answers given

**Reference:** Disco Elysium, Braid, The Stanley Parable

## 7. CORE LOOPS

### Player Journey Loop
```
1. Arrive in new universe
2. Meet NPCs and have conversations
3. Make choices that create Echoes
4. Collect memories
5. Form understanding
6. Travel to next universe or revisit with new context
7. Repeat until all connections made
```

### Conversation Loop
```
1. Initiate dialogue
2. Read character's line + context
3. Choose response (3-5 options)
4. See consequence (immediate + future)
5. Gain Echo fragment
6. Option to continue or end conversation
```

## 8. ENDINGS & VARIABLES

### Ending Calculation

Based on:
- **Relationship Scores** (with key NPCs across all universes)
- **Echo Collection** (which stories you prioritized)
- **Philosophical Choices** (how you interpret meaning)

### The 4 Ending Paths

1. **CONNECTOR ENDING**
   - Unlocked by: Deep relationships with many NPCs
   - Message: "Meaning is found in connection with others"
   - Epilogue: You see how your presence affected everyone

2. **ARCHITECT ENDING**
   - Unlocked by: Accepting your role in shaping events
   - Message: "We are all responsible for the world we create"
   - Epilogue: You understand the weight of your choices

3. **WITNESS ENDING**
   - Unlocked by: Observing without trying to change
   - Message: "Some things can't be changed, only understood"
   - Epilogue: You find peace in acceptance

4. **PARADOX ENDING**
   - Unlocked by: Contradictory choices / max chaos
   - Message: "Existence itself is paradoxical"
   - Epilogue: You embrace not knowing

### New Game+ Features

After first playthrough:
- Unlock "Omniscient Mode" — NPCs react to your future knowledge
- New dialogue options reflect what you've learned
- Hidden scenes become available
- Different path to subtle "true" ending

## 9. THEMES & PHILOSOPHY

### Central Questions

1. **"Can we change the past?"**
   - Game answer: No, but understanding reveals what was always true

2. **"Who am I across different timelines?"**
   - Game answer: You're the pattern, not the person

3. **"What makes life meaningful?"**
   - Game answer: What meaning do YOU choose to assign?

4. **"Are we free or bound by history?"**
   - Game answer: Both. Freedom is understanding your bonds.

### Philosophical Influences

- **Eternalism** (all times exist equally)
- **Existentialism** (meaning is what you create)
- **Determinism vs. Free Will** (explored, not resolved)
- **Panpsychism** (consciousness woven through time)
