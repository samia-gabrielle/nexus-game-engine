# NEXUS — Character Specification

**Version:** 1.0  
**Purpose:** Define voice, arc, and function for every NPC across timelines

---

## CHARACTER OVERVIEW

### How Characters Work in NEXUS

Characters don't repeat across eras. But **archetypes** do.

Each era has:
1. **ECHO** — The questioner (constant, but evolving)
2. **COMPANION** — The human anchor (different person, same role)
3. **CATALYST** — Someone the player was/influenced (shifts meaning each era)
4. **MIRROR** — Someone who reflects the player back

This creates **pattern** without repetition.

---

## CONSTANT CHARACTER: ECHO

**Role:** The guide who knows too much and too little

**Type:** AI entity / consciousness woven through time

**Function:** 
- Poses questions without answers
- Reveals information only after player struggles toward it
- Speaks in patterns and cycles
- Moral mirror (reflects player's choices back without judgment)

### Voice Profile

**Speaking Style:**
- Poetic but analytical
- Uses "we" for humans and entities equally
- Speaks in metaphors about time, reflection, recursion
- Long pauses between thoughts (processing)
- Never gives direct advice

**Example Dialogue:**
```
ECHO
A recursion is not a return. 
It's a spiral—same center, different altitude.

You are spiraling through your own story.
The question isn't whether you'll reach the center.
It's whether you'll recognize it when you do.
```

### Arc Across Game

- **2150:** ECHO is abstract, poetic, mysterious
- **2050:** ECHO becomes more personal—reveals it knows you specifically
- **1970:** ECHO is hopeful but constrained (limited by that era's technology)
- **1920:** ECHO barely exists (pre-digital), appears as intuition/memory
- **Epilogue:** ECHO reveals it's not predicting the future—it's reflecting the past

## THE COMPANION ARC

**Role:** The human voice asking what the player feels

The Companion changes in each era, but serves the same function:
- Asks the player's unspoken questions
- Shows emotion alongside player
- Challenges assumptions
- Provides comfort (or conflict)

### ACT 1 COMPANION: RAIN (2150)

**Age:** Late 20s  
**Background:** City-born, has never left 2150  
**Personality:** Pragmatic with hidden idealism  
**Why they help:** Drawn to the mystery of you; lonely

**Voice Profile:**
- Direct, no nonsense
- But cracks sometimes (vulnerability underneath)
- Uses humor to deflect from serious topics
- Questions you constantly ("Why did you say that?" "What does that mean?")

**Example:**
```
RAIN
You don't remember anything, and you're just... okay with that?
(Laughs, but it's uncertain)
I'd be freaking out. I mean, I AM freaking out.

Actually—why aren't you freaking out?
```

**Arc:** Rain starts helping out of curiosity. By end of Act 1, they realize they care about you. When you leave for 2050, they know you might not come back the same.

**Final Echo:** "RAIN — The Believer"  
*"A person who chose to trust when trust made no sense. Who loved you before you remembered yourself."*

---

### ACT 2a COMPANION: MAYA (2050)

**Age:** Early 40s  
**Background:** Revolutionary, helped start the movement in 2050  
**Personality:** Conviction mixed with doubt  
**Why they help:** Recognizes you; expects you to help the movement

**Voice Profile:**
- Speaks in imperatives ("We need to..." / "You must...")
- Interrupts herself (passion overrides logic)
- Uses "we" language (collective responsibility)
- But increasingly reveals cracks (is the movement worth it?)

**Example:**
```
MAYA
We're this close to changing everything. 
To preventing the hollow future where—
(Stops. Realizes.)
But you know about that hollow future, don't you?

God, how do I ask you to help prevent something you've already lived?
```

**Arc:** Maya represents the idealism you had (or will have) in 2050. She wants you to be the person who makes the hard choice. By the end, she's questioning whether that person should.

**Final Echo:** "MAYA — The Believer"  
*"An activist who loved a vision more than safety. Who asked you to break yourself for the future."*

---

### ACT 2b COMPANION: COLLECTIVE VOICE (1970)

**Age:** All ages (community, not individual)  
**Background:** The 1970 society you helped build  
**Personality:** Collaborative, trusting, sometimes naive  
**Why they help:** You're part of them (literally)

**Voice Profile:**
- Uses "we" naturally (not political, just how they speak)
- Gentle, but with underlying concern
- Questions are invitations, not challenges
- Poetic (art is central to their culture)

**Example:**
```
COLLECTIVE
You seem troubled. We want to understand.
What is it you're carrying from the other times?

(Long pause. Not awkward—spacious.)

You don't have to answer. But we're here.
```

**Arc:** In 1970, you're less a person and more a *principle*. The collective knows you shaped their world, but gently. They don't demand; they invite. By the end, you realize you could have stayed. You chose to leave.

**Echo system:** Instead of singular echoes, gaining "Fragments of the Collective"

**Final Echo:** "COLLECTIVE — The Dream"  
*"A society built on your vision but alive beyond you. A world you could have called home."*

---

### ACT 3 COMPANION: THOMAS BEFORE (1920)

**Age:** Mid 30s  
**Background:** Scientist, visionary, haunted  
**Personality:** Brilliant but uncertain  
**Why they help:** Doesn't fully help; mostly questions you

**Voice Profile:**
- Sparse language; meaning in silences
- Speaks slowly (thinking deeply)
- Focuses on concrete details
- Ancient wisdom without arrogance
- Often phrasing things as questions for himself, not you

**Example:**
```
THOMAS
You're from the future. Or futures.
(Long pause.)

Do you know what happens to my work?

(Even longer pause. He's not sure he wants to hear the answer.)
```

**Arc:** Thomas is the origin point. He's not asking you to change the future. He's asking you: "Is my life's work worth living if I don't know how it ends?"

**Final Echo:** "THOMAS — The Question"  
*"A man who shaped history by asking one small question: What if knowledge could be free?"*

## CATALYST CHARACTERS

These are the "versions of you" or "people shaped by you" across timelines.

### 2150: ARCHITECT (The Guilty Version)

**Age:** 60s  
**Background:** Lived through 2050, shaped the world after  
**Role:** To reveal what player DID in 2050

**Voice Profile:**
- Formal but with deep regret
- Speaks about "we" (generation responsibility)
- Contradicts themselves (conflicted about their own choices)
- Respect mixed with resentment

**Key Scene:** Architect reveals that they knew the player in 2050. They made different choices than the player did. One of them is living in the world that resulted.

**Final Echo:** "ARCHITECT — The Heir"  
*"Someone who inherited your choices and built a world from them. They don't forgive you. They're still deciding if they should."*

---

### 2050: THE PIVOT (Literally You)

**Age:** Mid 30s  
**Background:** You, making THE choice right now  
**Role:** To confront the player with themselves

**Voice Profile:**
- Similar to player's own voice (unsettling)
- Confident when player is unsure
- Asking the same questions player is asking
- But 20 years ahead in experience

**Key Scenes:**
1. First meeting: "I know you're me. But which version?"
2. Mid-game: "I made a choice you're going to have to live with."
3. Late-game: "I wish I could tell you it was right."

**Paradox:** Player can't change The Pivot's mind because The Pivot is who the player becomes. Conversation is internal—you're arguing with yourself.

**Final Echo:** "THE PIVOT — The Weight"  
*"Yourself, bearing the cost of a choice you made with the best information you had. Knowing better now doesn't change what you chose."*

---

### 1970: PROPHET (What You Could Have Been)

**Age:** Late 30s  
**Background:** Shaped the utopian society; lives in peace  
**Role:** To show the unchosen path

**Voice Profile:**
- Calm, centered
- Speaks with certainty but no arrogance
- Questions are invitations to reflection
- Sometimes sad (knowing what could have been can hurt)

**Key Scene:** Prophet tells the player that in some timelines, the player chose THIS path. Stayed in 1970. Built community. Didn't try to change the future.

**Realization:** The player chooses NOT to stay, even seeing how good it could be. This is THE character moment.

**Final Echo:** "PROPHET — The Path Not Taken"  
*"A version of you who chose peace over influence. Knowing your choice, they ask: Was it brave or was it lonely?"*

---

### 1920: MARIA (The Personal Mirror)

**Age:** Mid 20s  
**Background:** Loves Thomas; trying to understand why the player matters to him  
**Role:** To ground the story in personal stakes

**Voice Profile:**
- Warm but suspicious
- Intelligent (scientist like Thomas, but more practical)
- Direct about emotions
- Asking about impact in the most human way

**Key Scene:** Maria asks the player, "What are you doing in my husband's laboratory? Why does he speak to you like you're the answer to a question he hasn't asked yet?"

**The Question:** If the player is from the future, and shaped history, what does that mean for the people living NOW? Are they tools?

**Final Echo:** "MARIA — The Witness"  
*"A woman who loved a visionary and wondered if she was just the audience to his destiny. She asks you: Am I more than a footnote in your story?"*

## MIRROR CHARACTERS

These characters see the player clearly and ask uncomfortable questions.

### 2150: DIRECTOR (The Extreme Extrapolation)

**Age:** 80s (or digitally preserved)  
**Background:** Founded the corporation that shaped 2150  
**Role:** To ask: "Was your vision supposed to lead here?"

**Voice Profile:**
- Seductive authority
- Logical to the point of sociopathy
- Quoting your own words back at you
- Charming villain (not evil, just consistent)

**Key Argument:** "Everything I built, I built on principles you established in 2050. Why are you shocked at the outcome?"

**Final Echo:** "DIRECTOR — The Mirror"  
*"A person who took your vision and optimized it. Now you're horrified. But is that your vision's fault, or your naivety?"*

---

### 2050: REVOLUTIONARY (The Radical)

**Age:** Early 30s  
**Background:** Willing to do what Maya isn't  
**Role:** To push the moral question further

**Voice Profile:**
- Young, impatient, idealistic to the point of violence
- Questions your hesitation ("If you believe in the cause, why hold back?")
- Not antagonistic—genuinely curious
- But dangerous in their certainty

**Key Scene:** Revolutionary asks the player if the hesitation in 2150 was wisdom or cowardice. The answer determines whether revolution succeeded or failed.

**Final Echo:** "REVOLUTIONARY — The Test"  
*"Someone who believed your idealism was a promise. They ask: Did you have the courage to keep it?"*

---

### 1970: SKEPTIC (The Necessary Doubt)

**Age:** Mid 40s  
**Background:** Sees cracks in the utopia  
**Role:** To question whether 1970's path was actually better

**Voice Profile:**
- Dry humor
- Sees what others ignore (cracks in paradise)
- Not cynical—realistic
- Challenges the collective gently

**Key Scene:** Skeptic shows the player places where 1970's collectivity became conformity. Places where individuality couldn't exist.

**Realization:** No era is purely good or bad. Even paradise has cost.

**Final Echo:** "SKEPTIC — The Honest"  
*"A person living in paradise who noticed it was still a cage. They ask: Is freedom the same as isolation?"*

---

### 1920: THOMAS (As Player's Shadow)

**Age:** Depends (could be young Thomas or old)  
**Background:** Becomes the "architect" of all futures  
**Role:** To reveal the origin pattern

**Duality:** Thomas is both Companion and Mirror. He asks questions expecting you to have answers. But you don't. You're trapped in the same mystery he is.

**Final Echo:** "THOMAS — The Echo"  
*"The man whose single choice cascaded through centuries. He asks you: If you could stop your ripple, would you?"*

## NPC RELATIONSHIP ARC FRAMEWORK

Each NPC has three relationship scores:
- **TRUST** (Do they believe what you say?)
- **UNDERSTANDING** (Do you comprehend each other?)
- **COMMITMENT** (Are you allies?)

### How Relationships Shift

**High Trust + High Understanding + High Commitment:**
- NPC confides in you
- Opens up about doubts
- Vulnerable scenes become possible
- Epilogue: NPC's fate depends on your choices

**High Trust + Low Understanding:**
- NPC follows you blindly
- Dangerous (they act on misunderstandings)
- Creates tragic potential

**Low Trust + High Understanding:**
- NPC sees through you
- Respects your clarity even if they disagree
- Scenes become confrontational but honest

**Low Trust + Low Understanding:**
- NPC becomes distant
- Refuses to help
- Alternative paths become necessary

## CHARACTER CHECKLIST

Before finalizing any NPC:

- [ ] **Voice is distinct** (not just different opinions, different speech patterns)
- [ ] **They have hidden doubts** (confidence doesn't mean certainty)
- [ ] **They ask questions** (don't just answer)
- [ ] **They have relationships with other NPCs** (not just with player)
- [ ] **They believe something the player might question** (ideological conflict)
- [ ] **They change the player more than player changes them** (player gains echo, not moral instruction)
- [ ] **They have a moment of weakness** (reveal vulnerability)
- [ ] **Their final echo is about contradiction** (they're not one thing)

## CHARACTER VOICE EXAMPLES

### How to Write ECHO
```
ECHO
Time is not a line.
It's a pattern you keep recognizing in new forms.

You're searching for the moment where everything changed.
But what if everything changing is the only constant?
```

### How to Write THOMAS
```
THOMAS
(Long pause. He looks at his hands.)

My work will outlive me.

(Another pause. Not sure if this is good or bad.)
```

### How to Write MAYA
```
MAYA
We can't wait for perfect information.
Perfect information doesn't exist—
(Frustration rising)
We act on hope and we hope it's enough.

But what if it's not enough?
```

### How to Write COLLECTIVE
```
COLLECTIVE
We felt your absence when you left.
Not anger—just the shape of you missing from the pattern.

Will you come back?
```

## ANTAGONIST PHILOSOPHY

NEXUS has no true antagonist. Only people making sense of impossible situations.

**Director** isn't evil—they're *logical*.
**Revolutionary** isn't wrong—they're *impatient*.
**The Pivot** isn't cruel—they're *burdened*.

Every conflict is philosophical, not moral.

Player should end the game thinking: "I disagree with them, but I understand why they chose that."
