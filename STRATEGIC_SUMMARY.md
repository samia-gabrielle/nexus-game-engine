## THE PHILOSOPHICAL CORE

### Central Question (Refined)
**Old:** "What is my impact on humanity?"  
**New:** "What did I leave behind—and who had to live with it?"

**Why this matters:** Shifts focus from grand impact to intimate consequence. The game is not about saving the world. It's about understanding what the world became.

### Three Pillars

1. **Legacy** — Impact, not intention
   - Not about what you tried to do
   - About what actually changed because you existed
   - Small moments matter more than grand gestures

2. **Echo** — The pattern across time
   - Characters don't repeat; contradictions do
   - An idea cascades through four eras, changing form
   - Each era shows a different outcome of the same philosophy

3. **Ambiguity** — Victory is living with paradox
   - No ending is purely good or bad
   - Player should never have certainty their legacy was positive
   - Four endings, all equally valid, none definitively "true"

### Core Loop
```
Arrive in era → Meet someone who knew you → 
Discover what you did here → Make choice about it →
```

## THE ECHO SYSTEM

### What It Is
Echoes are not collectibles. They are **semantic fragments of meaning**.

Each echo captures:
- What a character believed about you
- How your presence changed their life
- The contradiction between their intention and outcome

### How It Works
- Meet NPC → Gain Echo: "CHARACTER — Descriptor"
- Combine echoes across eras → Unlock Memory (cutscene)
- Memory reveals deeper patterns and contradictions
- Player constructs their own understanding through accumulation

### Example Chain
```
Echo: "THOMAS — The Liberator" (1920)
  + Echo: "ALICE — The Revolutionary" (1970)
  + Echo: "CORPORATION — The Monument" (2050)
  = MEMORY: "The Cascade"
  (Shows how one idea became three different things)
```

## NARRATIVE PILLARS

### Principle 1: Every Choice Has Cost
No "right" answer. Honesty costs comfort. Action costs understanding.

### Principle 2: Echoes Complicate, Not Explain
An echo should make player think: "Wait, so it was BOTH good AND bad?"

### Principle 3: The Past Doesn't Change
Visiting earlier eras reveals what was always true. It doesn't alter history.

### Principle 4: Small Moments Matter Most
Game is about whether your presence at a specific table with a specific person mattered.

### Principle 5: Ambiguity Is Victory
If ending feels clear, you failed. It should feel true AND incomplete.

   - Mitigation: Reference VOICE PROFILE section constantly
